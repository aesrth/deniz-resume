01

About

I'm an economist and regulatory consultant with a PhD in Economics and more than a decade of independent advisory work on network industries — the energy, transport, and infrastructure systems that societies depend on. My work sits at the intersection of regulation, market design, and decarbonisation, drawing on deep knowledge of Türkiye's institutional environment and its links to European policy.

Across technical-assistance and advisory projects — for bodies including the EBRD, Turkish ministries, and EU research consortia — I've supported regulatory readiness assessments, carbon pricing and decarbonisation analysis, feasibility studies, and multi-stakeholder capacity-building programmes. I combine rigorous quantitative work (econometric modelling of large datasets in Python) with the practical craft of policy: engaging stakeholders, facilitating workshops, and turning analysis into recommendations decision-makers can act on.

Alongside consultancy, I helped establish the Istanbul Centre for Regulation (IC4R) and served as managing editor of the peer-reviewed journal Competition and Regulation in Network Industries, working closely with the Florence School of Regulation.

Market Design & Mechanisms

Decarbonisation Strategy

Data Analysis & Modelling
